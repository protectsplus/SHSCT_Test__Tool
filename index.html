Section 3 — Ligature point assessment (room by room)
Procedure Section 5: work up-down / left-right from a defined point in each room. Classify / colour-code each room. L = Likelihood (1–5) · I = Impact (1–5) · RR = L × I. If weight-bearing status unsure, assume yes.

ⓘ Complete suspension is not required for a ligature to be fatal. Partial suspension (semi-seated, kneeling, lying) carries the same risk. Assess all potential anchor points regardless of height. Some points (e.g. grab rails) may need to remain — formally assess and record the justification.

Section 4 — Overall risk rating of this ward / care facility

Determined after all ligature points and control measures considered. RED or AMBER requires ligature rescue cutters and staff training in that area.

■ RED — High risk
Care frequently delivered to patients with acute / severe mental illness, unpredictable behaviour. Most patients spend time in private without direct staff supervision.
■ AMBER — Medium risk
Most patients spend long periods with minimum direct supervision, usually in peer company. A number of unlocked and accessible areas present.
■ GREEN — Low risk
Areas with good movement of staff and patients through well-observed, high-traffic spaces.

⚠ Ligature rescue cutters required. Staff training must be arranged.

RED: all core staff must attend accredited half-day Ligature Rescue training (2-yearly refresher). Minimum 2 trained staff on duty at all times.
AMBER: designated staff attend half-day face-to-face training; remaining staff complete e-learning (3-yearly).
Submit training needs form to: safetyintervention.training@southerntrust.hscni.net
